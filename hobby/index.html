What is Laser Art?

Laser art is a unique form of visual expression, where light is used to paint the air in stunning, three-dimensional displays.

Who is Behind Laser Art?

Laser art, to me, is more than just a hobby; it’s a way to explore and express the unseen dimensions of light and space.

Inspired by artists like Laser Shark and Pretty Lights, I began experimenting with laser projections in my teens.

Previous Work

My journey with laser art has taken me to a range of events, from underground raves to more intimate art installations.

I’ve showcased work at festivals, collaborated with musicians, and experimented with projection mapping at venues.

Applications of Laser Art

The versatility of laser art makes it suitable for a variety of settings, from immersive music festivals to atmospheric installations.

It’s also used in education, planetariums, interactive exhibits, and advertising displays.

How It All Started

My fascination with lasers began in my early teens, starting with handheld laser pointers and evolving to full projections.

Each experiment taught me new ways to bend light into art, transforming simple beams into complex patterns.

Why Lasers?

Lasers represent the perfect blend of science and art, embodying both precision and unpredictability.
